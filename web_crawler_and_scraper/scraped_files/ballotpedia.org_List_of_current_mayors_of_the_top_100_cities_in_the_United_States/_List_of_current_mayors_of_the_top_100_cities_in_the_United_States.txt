<p>This page lists the <b>current mayors of the <a href="/Largest_cities_in_the_United_States_by_population" title="Largest cities in the United States by population">100 largest U.S. cities</a> by population</b>. As of 2020, 64,537,560 individuals lived in these cities, accounting for 19.47 percent of the nation's total population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the top 100 cities, there are 47 <a href="/Mayor-council_government" title="Mayor-council government">strong mayor governments</a>, 46 <a href="/Council-manager_government" title="Council-manager government">council-manager governments</a>, six <a href="#List_of_mayors">hybrid governments</a>, and one <a href="/City_commission" title="City commission">city commission</a>.
</p><p>As of December 2024, the <a href="/Party_affiliation_of_the_mayors_of_the_100_largest_cities" title="Party affiliation of the mayors of the 100 largest cities">partisan breakdown</a> of the mayors of the 100 largest U.S. cities is 63 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Democrats" title="Democrats">Democrats</a>, 25 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Republicans" title="Republicans">Republicans</a>, one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Libertarian" title="Libertarian">Libertarian</a>, four <a href="/Independent" title="Independent">independents</a>, and five <a href="/Nonpartisan" title="Nonpartisan">nonpartisans</a>. Two mayors' partisan affiliations are unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Based on 2020 population estimates, 76% of the population of the top 100 cities lived in cities with Democratic mayors, and 16% lived in cities with Republican mayors at the start of 2024.
</p><p>As of 2024, <a href="/Allen_Joines" title="Allen Joines">Allen Joines</a> (D), mayor of <a href="/Winston-Salem,_North_Carolina" title="Winston-Salem, North Carolina">Winston-Salem, North Carolina</a>, had been in office the longest. He first took office in 2001.

<p>The following table lists the current mayors of the top 100 U.S. cities by population, including the year each mayor took office, when the current term expires, and the city's type of government. Each type of government has a different distribution of power and different responsibilities between the mayor and the city council. The types of government are:
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/Mayor-council_government" title="Mayor-council government">Strong mayor</a>:</b> The city council serves as the city's primary legislative body, and the mayor serves as the city's chief executive.</li>
<li><b><a href="/Council-manager_government" title="Council-manager government">Council-manager</a>:</b> An elected city council—which includes the mayor and serves as the city's primary legislative body—appoints a chief executive called a city manager to oversee day-to-day municipal operations and to implement the council's policy and legislative initiatives.</li>
<li><b><a href="/City_commission" title="City commission">City commission</a>:</b> A city council, composed of an elected mayor and a board of elected commissioners, serves as the city's primary legislative and administrative body.</li>
<li><b>Hybrid:</b> The city council serves as the city's primary legislative body, and the mayor serves as the city's chief executive. The mayor, however, appoints a city manager to oversee the city's day-to-day operations and to implement city policies.</li></ul>
