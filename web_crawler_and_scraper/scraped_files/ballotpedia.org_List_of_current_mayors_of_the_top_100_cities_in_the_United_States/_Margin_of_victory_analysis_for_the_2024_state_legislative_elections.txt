<p>After all states release final certified results, this page will analyze the electoral <a>margins of victory</a> for state legislative seats up for election in 2024. A margin of victory is the difference between the share of votes cast for the winning candidate and the second-place candidate in an election.
</p><p>The analysis will show the average margin of victory in each state legislative upper chamber and lower chamber, breakdown margins of victory in each chamber by partisan affiliation, and list the state legislative seats that were won by fewer than 10 percentage points and fewer than 0.5 percentage points.

<h2><span>Analysis for 2022</span></h2>
<p>On November 8, 2022, 88 of the nation's 99 state legislative chambers held <a>regularly-scheduled elections</a> for 6,278 seats, meaning that 85.0% of all state legislative seats were up for election.
</p><p><a>Democrats</a> gained control of four chambers—the Michigan House and Senate, Minnesota Senate, and Pennsylvania House—bringing their total to 40. The changes in Minnesota and Michigan created Democratic <a>trifectas</a>. <a>Republicans</a> lost control of those five chambers, bringing their total to 57. In Alaska, a bipartisan coalition gained control of the Senate. The coalition in the House changed from being made primarily of Democrats and independents to one made primarily of Republicans.
</p><p>Across all 6,278 seats up for election, the average margin of victory was <b>27.7%</b>. An electoral <a><b>margin of victory</b> (MOV)</a> is the difference between the share of votes cast for the winning candidate and the second-place candidate in an election. Only races with more than one candidate were included in this average.
</p>
<div> <b>HIGHLIGHTS</b></div>
<div><li>Major-party candidates won 875 seats by margins of 10% or less; 471 of those seats were won by Democrats and 404 by Republicans.</li></div>
<div><li>The average margin of victory was 26.8% for Democrats and 28.6% for Republicans.</li></div><div><li>The chamber with the smallest average margin of victory in 2022 was the <a>New Hampshire House of Representatives</a> (10.6%), followed by the <a>South Dakota House of Representatives</a> (12.3%) and the <a>Arizona House of Representatives</a> (12.4%).</li></div>
<p>On this page, you will find an overview of average margins of victory by chamber, a detailed breakdown of each legislative chamber's margins of victory, and a closer look at the races decided by margins of 0.5% or smaller.
</p><p>To learn more about margins of victory in the previous even-year state legislative elections (2020), <b><a>click here</a></b>. That year, across the 5,875 seats up for election, the average margin of victory was 27.0%. Major-party candidates won 928 seats by margins of 10% or less—444 of those seats were won by Democrats and 484 by Republicans.
</p>

<h2><span>Analysis for 2020</span></h2>
<p>On November 3, 2020, 86 of the nation's 99 state legislative chambers held <a>regularly-scheduled elections</a> for 5,875 seats, meaning that 79.6% of all state legislative seats were up for election.
</p><p>Republicans won new majorities in two chambers: the <a>New Hampshire State Senate</a> and <a>New Hampshire House of Representatives</a>. There were no chambers where Democrats won new majorities in 2020. In one other chamber, the <a>Alaska House of Representatives</a>, a <a>minority coalition</a> remained in control.
</p><p>Across all 5,875 seats up for election, the average margin of victory was <b>27.0%</b>. An electoral <a><b>margin of victory</b> (MOV)</a> is the difference between the share of votes cast for the winning candidate and the second-place candidate in an election. Only races with more than one candidate were included in this average.
</p>
<div> <b>HIGHLIGHTS</b></div>
<div><li>Major-party candidates won 928 seats by margins of 10% or less; 444 of those seats were won by Democrats and 484 by Republicans.</li></div>
<div><li>The average margin of victory was 26.7% for Democrats and 27.3% for Republicans.</li></div><div><li>The chamber with the smallest average margin of victory in 2020 was the <a>New Hampshire House of Representatives</a> (9.1%), followed by the <a>Vermont State Senate</a> (11.1%) and the <a>South Dakota House of Representatives</a> (11.3%).</li></div>
