<p><b>Mattie Parker</b>  is the <a>Mayor of Fort Worth</a> in Texas. Parker assumed office on June 15, 2021. Parker's current term ends in 2025.</p><p>Parker   ran for re-election for <a>Mayor of Fort Worth</a> in Texas. Parker won  in the general election on <a>May 6, 2023</a>. </p>
<p>While mayoral elections in Fort Worth are nonpartisan, media outlets have reported that Parker is affiliated with the <a>Republican Party</a>.
</p>

<h2><span>Biography</span></h2>
<p>Mattie Parker earned a B.A. in government from the University of Texas at Austin and a law degree from Texas A&amp;M University. Parker's career experience includes working as the founding CEO of Fort Worth Cradle to Career and the Tarrant To &amp; Through (T3) Partnership, the chief of staff for the Fort Worth mayor and city council, and an attorney.
</p>

</p><h4><span>Campaign website</span></h4>
<p>Parker’s campaign website stated the following:
</p>
<div>
<table>
<tbody><tr>
<td>“
</td>
<td>
<ul><li><b>POSITIVE POLICIES, NOT DIVISIVE PARTISAN POLITICS.</b></li></ul>
<p>Mattie Parker knows it takes principled leaders, not politicians, to provide a safer and stronger Fort Worth. She will make sure the Mayor’s policies remain nonpartisan because the last thing our city needs is Washington-style division that pits neighbor against neighbor. The best way to lead our great city is with honesty and a commitment to bringing people together from all walks of life to develop solutions for our most pressing issues.
</p>
<ul><li><b>INCLUSIVE LEADERSHIP THAT LEAVES NO FAMILY BEHIND.</b></li></ul>
<p>As Fort Worth Mayor, Mattie will focus on helping every community in every part of our city thrive. She believes all families regardless of zip code should have the same opportunity for prosperity and safety. This requires focusing on aging neighborhoods that could use a boost with more jobs, better roads to reduce traffic, career training for our next generation and classrooms of excellence for all students.
</p>
<ul><li><b>A TAXPAYER WATCHDOG TO KEEP CITY HALL EFFICIENT.</b></li></ul>
<p>During her tenure as Chief of Staff to Mayor Betsy Price, Mattie served as the go-to leader to improve efficiency at City Hall and develop innovative cost-saving measures to save tax dollars. As our next Mayor, she will continue these efforts to protect taxpayers and look for additional ways to provide property tax relief for homeowners. This requires focusing our city budget on the basic services that have the biggest impact on our lives.
</p>
<ul><li><b>SAFER NEIGHBORHOODS WITH WELL-FUNDED POLICE SERVICES.</b></li></ul>
<p>Mattie will support strong police and fire services and push back against any efforts to defund our police force. Only with well-funded law enforcement can we best protect our neighborhoods and schools from violent crime. She also will work with non-profits and education leaders to ensure more students have the job training they need to succeed in today’s workforce – giving young adults real world skills and hope for a brighter future.
</p><p><b>Protecting our neighborhoods</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Serve as a careful steward and watchdog of our tax dollars</li>
<li>Introduce state-of-the-art technology and training for Fort Worth police and fire</li>
<li>Encourage frequent and consistent conversations between police and neighborhoods</li>
<li>Advocate for responsible oversight of our Crime Control Prevention District to make sure our public safety resources are focused in areas with the most need</li>
<li>Create and maintain quality and convenient city parks and recreation areas for all neighborhoods</li></ul>
<p><b>Inclusive leadership</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Work with health officials to secure access to quality healthcare for all neighborhoods, including COVID-19 vaccines and treatment</li>
<li>Connect all neighborhoods to jobs, schools and health centers with affordable and convenient transportation</li>
<li>Create public-private support and assimilation services for the homeless</li>
<li>Establish new partnerships to eliminate food deserts from all Fort Worth neighborhoods</li>
<li>Ensure that all neighborhoods have quality streets, sidewalks and streetlights</li></ul>
<p><b>Innovative problem solver</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Create public-private cooperation to attract new businesses for quality jobs for all Fort Worth citizens, especially low income areas that could use a boost</li>
<li>Cut red-tape from City Hall to encourage small business growth</li>
<li>Connect City Hall with neighborhood schools to create collegiate and vocational learning opportunities for all Fort Worth public schools</li>
<li>Establish Fort Worth as a new hub for small business innovation and opportunity</li>
<li>Connect Fort Worth parents to early childhood education resources for all Fort Worth children</li></ul>
<p><b>Responsive to all citizens of Fort Worth</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Promote a more inclusive approach so all citizens feel welcome to offer input into the future of our city</li>
<li>Schedule City government meetings in all council districts in our community</li>
<li>Maintain an open door to the Mayor’s office at all times</li>
<li>Require all city departments to adopt a customer-first environment at City Hall</li>
<li>Will regularly attend neighborhood meetings with Police Chief</li>
<li>Work closely with neighborhood leaders who best understand the unique issues facing their community</li></ul>
<p><b>Growing our economy through innovation</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Focus economic development efforts on competing in a global economy, aggressively pursuing the next corporate relocation opportunity for Fort Worth</li>
<li>Embrace and support entrepreneurs to help grow our economy and develop new, high-paying jobs</li>
<li>Make Fort Worth the next great American success story using a shared vision of prosperity to shape our economic future.</li>
<li>Invest in our students and young adults to prepare them for the workforce of tomorrow and attract and retain the best and brightest in our community</li>
<li>Continue supporting a business-friendly climate at City Hall that enables our businesses to grow and flourish.</li></ul>
<p>
</p>
