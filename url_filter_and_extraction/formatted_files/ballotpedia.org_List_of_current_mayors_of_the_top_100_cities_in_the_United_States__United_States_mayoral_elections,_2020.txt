Here is a summary of key 2020 mayoral battleground elections:</p><h4><span>Mayoral election in Miami-Dade County, Florida</span></h4><dl><dd><dl><dd><i>See also:<a>Mayoral election in Miami-Dade County, Florida (2020)</a></i></dd></dl></dd></dl><p><a>Daniella Levine Cava</a>defeated<a>Esteban Bovo Jr.</a>in the nonpartisan general election for Mayor of<a>Miami-Dade County</a>on November 3, 2020. Incumbent Mayor<a>Carlos Gimenez</a>reached his consecutive term limit and could not run for re-election. Though the race was nonpartisan, the candidates received partisan support. Four local Republican organizations endorsed Bovo, who held office in the<a>Florida House of Representatives</a>as a Republican from 2008 to 2012. Seven local and state Democratic organizations, including the<a>Florida Democratic Party</a>, endorsed Levine Cava. The office was last held by a Democratic-aligned candidate in 2004, which was also the last time a Democratic-aligned candidate and a Republican-aligned candidate faced off in a general election.</p><h4><span>Mayoral election in Portland, Oregon</span></h4><dl><dd><dl><dd><i>See also:<a>Mayoral election in Portland, Oregon (2020)</a></i></dd></dl></dd></dl><p>Incumbent<a>Ted Wheeler</a>defeated<a>Sarah Iannarone</a>and<a>Teressa Raiford</a>(write-in) in the general election for mayor of<a>Portland, Oregon</a>on November 3, 2020. Wheeler said he led on police reform and the city's COVID-19 response. His campaign website said, "We are in the midst of a pandemic, the ensuing economic crisis, racial justice reckoning, and facing the constantly growing effects of climate change. We have an incredible amount of work ahead, and need continued leadership now more than ever." Iannarone's campaign website said, "The current Mayor’s record is full of broken promises on solutions for houselessness and inequality. He’s shown no leadership on civil unrest or public safety. ... I’m offering a progressive alternative."</p><h4><span>Mayoral election in Stockton, California</span></h4><dl><dd><dl><dd><i>See also:<a>Mayoral election in Stockton, California (2020)</a></i></dd></dl></dd></dl><p><a>Kevin Lincoln</a>defeated incumbent<a>Michael Tubbs</a>in the general election for mayor of<a>Stockton, California</a>, on November 3, 2020. Tubbs conceded the race on November 17. The general election was held after no candidate received a majority of the vote in the March 3 primary. Mayoral elections in Stockton are nonpartisan, meaning that candidates’ party affiliations do not appear on the ballot. However, Lincoln was a member of the Republican Party at the time of the election and Tubbs was a member of the Democratic Party. Tubbs' campaign website said he was "running to continue revitalizing our neighborhoods, attract more good paying jobs, increase safety through community policing, and bring innovation into our civic life and local economy." According to the<i>Los Angeles Times</i>, Tubbs was "the city’s first Black mayor and a favorite in progressive circles for championing ideas such as universal basic income." Lincoln's campaign website included proposals for a homeless policy initiative, public safety policy initiative, and civic engagement plan. Lincoln received support from local police and firefighters unions. His professional experience included serving in the Marines, working in private security, and pastoring a church.</p><h4><span>Mayoral election in El Paso, Texas</span></h4><dl><dd><dl><dd><i>See also:<a>Mayoral election in El Paso, Texas (2020)</a></i></dd></dl></dd></dl><p><a>Oscar Leeser</a>defeated incumbent<a>Donald "Dee" Margo</a>in the runoff election for mayor of<a>El Paso, Texas</a>, on December 12, 2020. Leeser received 79.5% of the vote to Margo's 20.5%. The runoff election was held after no candidate received a majority of the vote in the November 3 general election. Mayoral elections in El Paso are nonpartisan, meaning that candidates’ party affiliations do not appear on the ballot. However, media outlets identified Leeser as a member of the Democratic Party, and Margo previously served as a Republican in the<a>Texas House of Representatives</a>. Leeser served as mayor of El Paso from 2013 to 2017. He did not seek re-election in 2017, and Margo won a<a>runoff election</a>for the office. According to<i>The Texas Tribune</i>, the 2020 runoff election was "dominated by Margo’s management of the [COVID-19] crisis." According to the<i>Tribune</i>, "Margo ran on his experience guiding the city through three major crises — not just the pandemic, but also the 2019 massacre at a Walmart and the Central American migrant influx prior to that," while Leeser "pitched himself as a better crisis manager who would do more to bring the city together to get the virus under control."</p><h2><span>Mayoral partisanship</span></h2><dl><dd><dl><dd><i>See also:<a>Partisanship in United States municipal elections (2020)</a></i></dd></dl></dd></dl><p>Once mayors elected in 2020 assumed office, the mayors of 65 of the country's 100 largest cities were affiliated with the<a>Democratic Party</a>.</p><p>Mayoral elections were held in 29 of the 100 largest U.S. cities in 2020. Seven party changes took place as a result of these 29 elections. Five offices held by Republican incumbents and two offices held by Democratic incumbents changed partisan control.</p><p>In<b><a>Scottsdale, Arizona</a></b>, independent<a>David Ortega</a>won the open seat. Incumbent<a>Jim Lane</a>(R) was term-limited. In<b><a>Irvine, California</a></b>, Democrat<a>Farrah Khan</a>defeated incumbent<a>Christina Shea</a>(R). In<b><a>San Diego, California</a></b>, Democrat<a>Todd Gloria</a>won the open seat. The incumbent,<a>Kevin Faulconer</a>(R), was term-limited. In<b><a>Stockton, California</a></b>, Republican<a>Kevin Lincoln II</a>defeated incumbent<a>Michael Tubbs</a>(D). In<b><a>Honolulu, Hawaii</a></b>, independent<a>Rick Blangiardi</a>won the open seat. Democratic mayor<a>Kirk Caldwell</a>was term-limited. In<b><a>El Paso, Texas</a></b>, Democrat<a>Oscar Leeser</a>defeated incumbent<a>Donald Margo</a>(R). In<b><a>Corpus Christi, Texas</a></b>, nonpartisan<a>Paulette Guajardo</a>defeated incumbent<a>Joe McComb</a>(R).</p><h2><span>Mayoral elections across the United
